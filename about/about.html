Personal Statement

As students receiving a formal education, we are focused on a very specific kind of knowledge: factual, academic information we learn from our teachers and books. Sometimes this focus causes us to forget all the other kinds of knowledge essential for a good life. In addition to the intellectual progress I make in school, I try to recognize my personal progress.

In my experience, it is more difficult to get to know oneself than it is to memorize new information. It takes many different skills to maintain motivation and express empathy. Because I am not a person who is focused on the result, I am interested in creative challenges. When I am drawing, I feel a sense of comfort and excitement. I have some familiar tools to work with- the skills I have learned and the materials I like- but each new project is unknown. In fact, I believe I have the most precious and lucky thing in life: something I love to do.

When I made my first three-dimensional work, I was first very aware of all the ways I felt challenged by the new factors I did not consider in two-dimensional works. With three-dimensional work, you must consider the space around the work as well as the work itself. This was challenging, but I slowly found my intuition. In the process of making a paper sculpture, I saw how beautiful the light passed through the spaces between the paper and I realized how exciting this interaction is between natural light and three-dimensional objects. This was a lesson I cherished, however unsuccessful the work looked at the end.

I am also a person who is encouraged by healthy relationships with my teachers and peers. I value strong communication and find that my conversations are a source of confidence and meaning. In traditional Chinese schools, it is unusual to question your teacher or ask for help. Instead, you are expected to take notes during lectures and avoid bothering the teacher. But, when I switched to a new school, I began to understand how powerful it is to learn consistent and open communication. My teachers encourage me to ask questions and tell them how I am progressing with my coursework. They do not blame me for my misunderstandings as long as I have taken the time to ask thoughtful questions.

Communication with others allows me to have a deep understanding of other thoughts. This builds on though my life and increased my ability of empathy at the same time. Whenever there is a conflict happening between my friends and me, I will always be trying to put myself into her shoes and listening to her argument. By doing this, I realize what is the difference between our mind set which lead to this conflict. From sensing their emotion, I can point out my mistake, solve the conflict quickly, and provide it from happening for the next time.

The truth is that I have seen people graduate from high school and worry they did not choose the right subject or the best university. What is the point in getting full marks if you cannot identify what you love? While academic concepts are often forgotten after school finishes, our personal development continues. Knowing myself and knowing what I want, is much more important than full marks. At the end of the day, I want to have a life in which it is not relevant to consider any regrets. I accept that I will make mistakes and I accept that I will face challenges, but I do not accept a life without integrity and kindness.
